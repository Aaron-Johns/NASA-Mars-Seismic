Mars Seismic Waves

Seismic activity on Mars, known as "Marsquakes," was detected by NASA's InSight mission. These quakes reveal important details about the planet's inner structure, including its core and crust composition.

Marsquakes are weaker than earthquakes, but their patterns help scientists understand the geological history of Mars. Unlike Earth, Mars doesn't have tectonic plates, so these quakes occur due to the planet's cooling and contracting.
